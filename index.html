Nature Methods Cover Illustration

Print Design | Artwork

For the March 2025 cover of Nature Methods, I created an illustration to accompany the publication “Segment Anything for Microscopy” by Archit et al., which explores the application of foundation models for segmentation in high-resolution microscopy.

The cover visual features a stylized depiction of various subcellular structures, including a mitochondrion, Golgi apparatus, and other microscopic elements. The aesthetic merges clarity with abstraction—each structure is overlaid with segmented color masks representing the varying levels of segmentation success achieved by the method. This intentional layering visually communicates both the power and limitations of automated segmentation in complex biological data.

To make the subject approachable and visually engaging, I opted for a soft, pastel-inspired palette and simplified organic forms. These design choices aim to balance scientific relevance with visual appeal, helping to bridge the gap between computational analysis and biological intuition. The composition invites viewers to consider both the beauty and intricacy of cellular data, aligning with the cover’s editorial goal of accessible and compelling science communication.

  • Client: Campus Institute for Data Science / Computational Cell Analytics Group
  • Role: Grafikdesign
  • Publication: Archit et al., “Segment Anything for Microscopy”, Nature Methods (2025)
  • Link: nature.com/articles/s41592-024-02580-4

Planto – Atlas der Gemüsekulturen

Editorial Design · Writing & Typesetting

With my first book Planto – Atlas der Gemüsekulturen, I wrote, designed, and fully typeset a book that serves as both a guide and an invitation to explore the diversity of edible plants. Targeted at hobby gardeners and anyone curious about growing their own food, the book aims to inspire without overwhelming—providing just enough practical knowledge to get started while leaving space for personal discovery.

Visually, the book embraces a clean and minimal design language, using generous white space, a calm typographic hierarchy, and a restrained color palette to create a sense of clarity and focus. The cover and interior layouts reflect the book’s philosophy: functional yet elegant, approachable but not simplistic.

This project combines editorial design, typography, and information structuring into a cohesive reading experience. Planto was created to be as enjoyable to leaf through on a winter morning as it is useful during planting season, bridging the gap between reference book and personal inspiration.

  • Date: 2021
  • Role: Author · Editorial Design · Typography & Typesetting · Production
  • Client: Self-publication
